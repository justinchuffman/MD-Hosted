# Meat&Veg Roasting Guide

**Oven temp: 400°F for everything.** One temperature, no fiddling.

**General rules:** Toss with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Single layer on the pan — crowding steams instead of roasts. Parchment paper for easy cleanup.

---

## Quick Reference

| Vegetable | Prep | Fresh Time | Frozen Time | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Broccoli** | Cut into florets | 20–25 min | 25–30 min | Don't thaw frozen — roast straight from the bag. Spread extra wide, frozen releases moisture. |
| **Sweet potatoes** | Peel, dice into 1-inch cubes | 30–35 min | 35–40 min | Smaller cubes = faster. Smoked paprika is great on these. |
| **Bell peppers** | Seed, cut into strips or chunks | 20–25 min | 25–30 min | Get a little char on the edges — that's the good part. |
| **Zucchini** | Cut into half moons or quarters lengthwise | 20–25 min | Not recommended | Frozen zucchini gets too watery. Use fresh. |
| **Red onion** | Peel, slice into wedges or thick rings | 25–30 min | — | Caramelizes beautifully. Pairs with everything. |
| **Cauliflower** | Cut into florets | 25–30 min | 30–35 min | Same rules as broccoli. Great with cumin or curry powder. |
| **Carrots** | Peel, cut into sticks or coins | 25–30 min | 30–35 min | Thicker cuts = more roast time. Halve baby carrots lengthwise. |
| **Asparagus** | Snap off woody ends | 12–15 min | 15–18 min | Thin spears cook faster. Check early. |
| **Green beans** | Trim ends | 15–20 min | 20–25 min | Frozen works fine — don't thaw, just toss on the pan. |
| **Brussels sprouts** | Halve, remove outer leaves | 25–30 min | 30–35 min | Cut side down on the pan for max caramelization. |
| **Butternut squash** | Peel, seed, dice into 1-inch cubes | 30–35 min | 35–40 min | Same timing as sweet potatoes. |
| **Cherry tomatoes** | Leave whole or halve | 15–20 min | — | Burst and get jammy. Great tossed into grain bowls. |
| **Potatoes (baby/red)** | Halve or quarter | 30–35 min | — | Use fresh. Toss with garlic powder. |
| **Mushrooms** | Quarter or halve (leave small ones whole) | 20–25 min | Not recommended | Need dry heat — frozen ones release too much water. |
| **Cabbage** | Cut into thick wedges or chunks | 25–30 min | — | Underrated roasted veg. Gets sweet and crispy at the edges. |

## Frozen Broccoli Tips (since you have three bags)

Frozen broccoli roasts well but needs a little extra attention compared to fresh:

- **Don't thaw it.** Straight from freezer to pan.
- **Spread it out more than you think.** Frozen releases moisture as it cooks — overcrowding = steaming, not roasting. Use two pans if needed.
- **Bump time up 5 min** vs. fresh, and check at 25 min. You want some browning on the edges.
- **Pat dry if you can be bothered.** Dumping frozen florets onto a towel and patting for 30 seconds removes surface ice and helps them crisp. Not required, but it helps.
- **Bump the oven to 425°F** if you want crispier results from frozen. The extra heat drives off the moisture faster.

## Protein at 400°F (same pan or second pan alongside veggies)

All of these work at 400°F so you can roast protein and veg at the same time without adjusting the oven.

| Protein | Prep | Time at 400°F | Internal Temp | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Chicken breast (boneless)** | Pound to even thickness or butterfly if thick. Season with olive oil + spices. | 20–25 min | 165°F | Add to pan when veggies in the 20–25 min group go in. Pull chicken when done, let veggies keep going if needed. |
| **Chicken thighs (boneless)** | Season, place skin-side up if skin-on. | 25–30 min | 165°F | More forgiving than breast — harder to overcook. Great with the 25–30 min veggie group. |
| **Salmon fillets** | Season, place skin-side down. | 12–15 min | 145°F | Goes in LAST. Start your veggies, then add salmon partway through. |
| **Cod / tilapia / white fish** | Season, pat dry first. | 12–15 min | 145°F | Same as salmon — add late. White fish is thinner so check at 10 min. |
| **Shrimp** | Peel, devein, toss with oil + seasoning. | 8–10 min | Pink and opaque | Goes in very last. Overcooks fast. |

**The trick for double duty:** Start your longest-cooking veggies first (sweet potatoes, potatoes, carrots). Add your protein when there's the right amount of time left on the clock. Add fast-cooking veggies (asparagus, green beans) at the same time as the protein or a few minutes before.

**Example timeline for a Sunday sheet pan:**
- 0 min: Sweet potatoes go in
- 10 min: Add broccoli, bell peppers, onion
- 15 min: Add chicken breast (needs ~20 min from here)
- 35 min: Everything comes out together

**Example timeline for Wednesday fish night:**
- 0 min: Potatoes go in
- 10 min: Add asparagus or green beans
- 15 min: Add fish
- 27–30 min: Everything comes out together

## How to Season for Roasting

**The method every time:** Add olive oil and all dry spices to the veggies in a bowl or directly on the pan. Toss until everything is evenly coated, then spread in a single layer. The oil helps the spices stick and promotes browning. Wet additions (lemon juice, soy sauce, etc.) go on *after* roasting — otherwise they steam the veg.

**Amounts for one sheet pan of vegetables (~4 servings):**
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 3/4 tsp salt
- 1/2 tsp pepper
- Then add your flavor profile below

### Flavor Profiles

Each profile lists a primary seasoning (the main flavor) and a secondary (the supporting note). Amounts are per sheet pan.

**Everyday — works on literally anything**
- Primary: 1 tsp garlic powder
- Secondary: 1 tsp paprika
- Good on: everything, every night, no thinking required

**Mediterranean**
- Primary: 1.5 tsp Italian seasoning (or 1/2 tsp each oregano, thyme, basil)
- Secondary: 1/2 tsp garlic powder
- Finish with: squeeze of lemon after roasting
- Good on: zucchini, bell peppers, broccoli, potatoes, chicken

**Smoky Southwest**
- Primary: 1 tsp smoked paprika
- Secondary: 1 tsp cumin + 1/4 tsp chili powder
- Finish with: squeeze of lime after roasting
- Good on: sweet potatoes, bell peppers, cauliflower, corn, black beans, ground turkey

**Warm Spice / North African**
- Primary: 1.5 tsp ras el hanout
- Secondary: 1/2 tsp cumin
- Finish with: drizzle of olive oil and a squeeze of lemon after roasting
- Good on: carrots, cauliflower, sweet potatoes, butternut squash, chickpeas, chicken

**Indian-Inspired**
- Primary: 1.5 tsp garam masala
- Secondary: 1/2 tsp turmeric + 1/4 tsp cumin
- Finish with: squeeze of lemon or dollop of yogurt
- Good on: cauliflower, potatoes, carrots, sweet potatoes, chicken

**Herby / French**
- Primary: 1 tsp herbes de Provence (or 1/2 tsp thyme + 1/2 tsp rosemary)
- Secondary: 1/2 tsp garlic powder
- Finish with: nothing needed, or a small drizzle of good olive oil
- Good on: potatoes, zucchini, mushrooms, carrots, chicken, white fish

**Asian-Inspired**
- Primary: 1 tbsp soy sauce + 1 tsp sesame oil — *toss on after roasting*
- Secondary: 1/2 tsp garlic powder + 1/2 tsp ground ginger (these go on before, with the olive oil)
- Finish with: sesame seeds, sliced green onion
- Good on: broccoli, green beans, mushrooms, carrots, salmon, chicken

**Simple Lemon Garlic**
- Primary: 1 tsp garlic powder
- Secondary: zest of 1 lemon (toss on before roasting)
- Finish with: juice of that lemon squeezed on after roasting
- Good on: asparagus, green beans, broccoli, salmon, white fish

**Spicy Honey (kid-friendly sweet heat)**
- Primary: 1 tsp paprika + 1/4 tsp cayenne (reduce cayenne for younger kids)
- Secondary: 1/2 tsp garlic powder
- Finish with: drizzle of honey after roasting (1–2 tbsp)
- Good on: sweet potatoes, carrots, chicken breast, salmon

## Timing Groups (for roasting multiple veggies on the same pan)

If you're doing a big batch on Sunday, group veggies with similar cook times together so nothing burns or stays raw:

**~15 min group:** Asparagus, green beans, cherry tomatoes

**~20–25 min group:** Broccoli, bell peppers, zucchini, mushrooms

**~25–30 min group:** Carrots, cauliflower, red onion, Brussels sprouts, cabbage

**~30–35 min group:** Sweet potatoes, butternut squash, potatoes

If you want to mix groups, add the longer-cooking veggies first, then add the faster ones partway through.
